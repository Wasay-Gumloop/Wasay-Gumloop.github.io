i'm not a [blank] person

on the sentences that quietly cage you.

we lean toward confirming what we already believe about ourselves. "i'm an introvert" stops being a description and starts being a fence. it declines the concert before you've thought about whether you wanted to go. it skips the dinner before you've met the people. repeat it enough and the sentence starts living your life for you.

i read an idea once about adding a mental asterisk to these sentences. not "i'm an introvert." instead, "i'm an introvert, but i could try*." the asterisk isn't hidden doubt. it's an opening, written into the sentence itself. you haven't reversed anything. you've just refused to let the sentence be the last word. most of the doors we close on ourselves were never actually locked, just labelled.

a learning, not a lesson. i still catch myself doing it most weeks.
