i'm not a [blank] person

on the sentences that quietly cage you.

the sentences we say about ourselves most casually are the ones that do the most damage. "i'm an introvert" is the one i've used for years. it sounds like a description. it works like a fence. someone asks if i want to go to a concert and the sentence is already in my mouth, doing the work of declining before i've even thought about whether i wanted to go. i'm an introvert, so i don't do concerts. i'm an introvert, so i don't do big dinners. i'm an introvert, so. the word stops being a thing i am and becomes a thing i hide behind, and the hiding gets more comfortable every time.

the move i'm trying to learn is small. add "for now" to the end. not "i'm an introvert, i don't do concerts." instead, "i'm an introvert, i don't do concerts for now..." the trailing dots are the whole point. they refuse to let the sentence close. they admit that the person saying it today might not be the person reading it back in a year. the scary part was never the concert or the dinner or whatever else i declined in a sentence i said too quickly. the scary part is realising how many doors i closed on myself with throwaway lines, and how few of them were ever actually locked.

a learning, not a lesson. i still catch myself doing it most weeks.
