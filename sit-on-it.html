how long you sit on it

Moving with speed solves more problems than i used to think.

recently i've been trying to embody one thing: moving with speed solves more problems than i used to think. not all of them, and not perfectly. but more.

what i actually mean is this. how long you sit on a problem is usually the problem. the stress isn't the unanswered message or the decision i keep circling. it's the waiting. the problem isn't growing in that time. the gap is.

i learned this trading. the loss was rarely the wrong call. it was hesitating, sitting on a position, waiting for a cleaner signal that never came. hesitation cost me more than being wrong ever did.

when i sit still, my head fills with worst cases. when i move, reality shows up and corrects me. reality is almost always kinder than the version in my head.

so the rule i've landed on is simple. if something's itching, scratch it. just get it over with.

a few things i'm trying to do more of:

  • treat stalling as a sign i already know the answer and i'm just buying time.
  • send it now. a bad reply beats an open loop.
  • pick momentum over perfect timing, every time.

still learning this one. but every time i move sooner than i want to, i'm glad i did.
